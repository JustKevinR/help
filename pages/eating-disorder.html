An eating disorder is a serious mental illness, characterised by eating, exercise and body weight or shape becoming an unhealthy preoccupation of someone's life.

Signs of an eating disorder:

  • Dramatic weight loss
  • Concern about eating in public
  • Preoccupation with weight, food, calories, fat grams, or dieting
  • Complaints of constipation, cold intolerance, abdominal pain or lethargy
  • Excuses to avoid mealtime
  • Intense fear of weight gain or being “fat”
  • Dressing in layers to hide weight loss or stay warm
  • Severely limiting and restricting the amount and types of food consumed
  • Refusing to eat certain foods
  • Denying feeling hungry

Tips to help your eating disorder recovery:

  • Give yourself unconditional permission to enjoy all foods
  • Work to ensure adequate nourishment from food
  • Work towards variety
  • Recognize and honor your hunger
  • Feel your fullness
  • Encourage satisfaction
  • Connect with your body in joyful, purposeful way
  • Stay appropriately hydrated
  • Cope with emotions
